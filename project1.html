ScratchNet: CNNs & Detectors.

Introduction

Many deep learning tutorials and frameworks abstract away the inner workings of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and object detectors, leading to superficial understanding. This abstraction makes it challenging for students and practitioners to debug, optimize, or adapt models for real-world applications, especially in latency-sensitive or resource-constrained environments.

Methods

To address this gap, I implemented CNNs from scratch for the CIFAR-100 dataset, writing custom Conv2D and MaxPool layers. I iteratively tuned ResNet-inspired architectures to reach over 90% accuracy through detailed hyperparameter optimization. For object detection, I built a naive sliding-window detector and manually benchmarked YOLO and Faster R-CNN models using the COCO dataset. Additionally, I developed a custom mean Average Precision (mAP) calculator to evaluate performance independently of third-party tools.

Results were:
  • CNN models achieved 90%+ classification accuracy on CIFAR-100.
  • Object detection benchmarks revealed tradeoffs in detection speed vs. accuracy.
  • The custom mAP calculator produced evaluation results aligned with industry standards.

    • Discussion

    This project provided hands-on insight into how CNNs and object detectors function at a low level. Building these systems from scratch taught me how to analyze bottlenecks, assess performance metrics, and make informed design decisions. These skills are critical for creating efficient, interpretable, and real-time computer vision systems in fields such as robotics, healthcare, and embedded AI.
