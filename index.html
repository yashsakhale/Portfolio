About me

I am currently in my final semester at Carnegie Mellon University pursuing M.S in Artificial Intelligence - Mechanical Engineering.

Professional Experience: I started my journey as an Artificial Intelligence Intern at Vigilant Inc., a fast-paced startup in Alexandria, Virginia. From day one, I was responsible for building core AI components from scratch: designing, developing, and deploying production-grade systems for real-world applications. My work spanned the full lifecycle: prototyping, model training, backend integration, and shipping AI solutions to production. My rapid impact and ability to deliver results led to my transition into a full-time AI Engineer role, where I continue to architect and scale advanced AI systems for the company.

Academic Research: I continue to be part of Carnegie Mellon University's Computational Engineering and Robotics Lab (CERLAB), working on my industry-sponsored research project involving simulating and modeling of LiDAR sensors (OS1 Ouster and Velodyne VLP), utilizing camera-lidar fusion and predictive models to filter dust particles from point cloud data for autonomous vehicles deployed on construction and mining sites.

Areas of Expertise: Deep learning architectures for Autonomous Vehicles, Generative AI, Large Language Models, MLOps workflows, Cloud computing (AWS), REST API development, and real-time data processing systems.
